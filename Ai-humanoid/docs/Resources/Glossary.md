# Glossary of Key Terms

This glossary explains important terms used throughout the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics course.

---

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)**  
The ability of machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence.

**Physical AI**  
AI systems that operate in the physical world, perceiving and acting on their environment.

**Embodied Intelligence**  
AI that learns by interacting with the physical environment, often through a robotic body.

**Humanoid Robot**  
A robot designed to resemble a human in form and/or behavior.

**ROS 2 (Robot Operating System)**  
A middleware platform for developing robot control software.

**Gazebo**  
A physics-based simulation environment for robots.

**Unity**  
A real-time 3D development platform used for robot visualization and simulation.

**NVIDIA Isaac Sim**  
A simulation tool for developing, training, and testing AI robots in photorealistic environments.

**VLA (Vision-Language-Action)**  
Integration of visual perception, natural language understanding, and robotic actions.

**Digital Twin**  
A virtual representation of a physical system for testing and simulation.

**SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)**  
A technique enabling robots to map their environment while tracking their location.

**URDF (Unified Robot Description Format)**  
A standard format for describing robot models in ROS.

**Capstone Project**  
The final project in the course combining all learned concepts into a functional system.

**Jetson Orin**  
NVIDIA edge AI platform used for deploying AI models on physical robots.

---

### Note
Familiarity with these terms is essential for understanding the concepts and exercises in this course.
