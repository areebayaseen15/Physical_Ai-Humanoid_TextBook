# Chapter 2 Specification: Simulating Physics, Gravity, and Collisions in Gazebo

## Book Context
- **Book Title:** Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics — An AI-Native Textbook for Panaversity  
- **Module:** 2 — The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity)  
- **Chapter:** 2 — Simulating Physics, Gravity, and Collisions in Gazebo  

## Objective
- Introduce students to physics simulation in Gazebo.  
- Teach gravity, collision handling, and basic robot-environment interaction.  
- Provide a foundation for subsequent sensor simulation and environment building.  

## Scope
- Digital Twin concepts and simulation importance.  
- Overview of Gazebo and supported physics engines.  
- Gravity and collision configuration for robots and environment.  
- Running simple Gazebo simulations with ROS 2 integration.  

## Audience
- Graduate-level robotics and AI students with basic Python knowledge.  

## Learning Outcomes
After completing this chapter, students should be able to:  
1. Explain the role of physics engines in Gazebo.  
2. Configure gravity and collision parameters in simulation.  
3. Run a basic robot simulation in Gazebo with ROS 2.  
4. Analyze robot behavior and interaction with environment.
